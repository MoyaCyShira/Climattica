TROPICAL CLIMATES

Tropical climates have cooler
temperatures due to their high altitude.

They receive more rainfall or
snowfall because of their elevation.

These climates are typically found in mountainous regions.

The weather in tropical highlands varies significantly from the surrounding lowland areas.

Proceed to Types of Tropical Climates ->

Rainforest

The tropical rainforest is a hot, moist biome where it rains all year long. It is known for its dense canopies of vegetation that form different layers. The top layer or canopy contains giant trees that grow to heights of 75 meters or more.

Grassland

Grasslands are generally open and continuous, fairly flat areas of grass. They are often located between temperate forests at high latitudes and deserts at subtropical latitudes.

Semi-Arid

A semi-arid climate is a dry climate sub-type. It is located on regions that receive precipitation below potential evapotranspiration, but not as low as a desert climate.

Desert

A desert is a landscape where little precipitation occurs and, consequently, living conditions create unique biomes and ecosystems.

SUBTROPICAL CLIMATES

Subtropical climates have high humidity and warmer temperatures due to the hot ocean currents and more sunlight than the rest of the Earth.

These climates are found in the
southeast part of most continents
where there is a body of water beside it.

Proceed to Types of Subtropical Climates ->

Subtropical Humid

Known for its high humidity levels due
to the direct sunlight and hot currents from a body of water. The average summer temperatures at 21°C to 26°C and winter temperatures at 7.5°C to 16°C.

Subtropical Desert

Known for its high humidity levels due
to the direct sunlight and hot currents from a body of water. The average summer temperatures at 21°C to 26°C and winter temperatures at 7.5°C to 16°C.

TEMPERATE CLIMATES

Temperate climates experience four distinct seasons, including hot summers and cold winters, as seen in cities like Chicago and Beijing.

In Geography, the temperate climates
of Earth occur in the middle latitudes

Temperate

Temperate climates have mild seasons but unpredictable weather.

Temperate zones cover 7% of the Earth's land but house about 40% of its population, thanks to mild climates, ample rain, and fertile soils.

Subarctic

Subarctic climates in high-latitude continents have the coldest winters and mild summers, with the largest temperature range on Earth. Found only in the Northern Hemisphere marked by extreme temperature shifts.

Temperate Grassland

Grasslands features tall grasses in humid areas and short grasses in dry, windy regions. Savannas experience wet and dry seasons, similar to the Veldt.

Temperate Desert

The desert biome features high summer temperatures and low winter temperatures. Cold winters with precipitation primarily from snowmelt.

POLAR CLIMATES

A polar climate is one in which there
are long, frigid winters and brief, cool
summers, and intense cold throughout the year.

Antarctica is a frozen continent at the Earth's South Pole, covered in ice and known for its extreme cold. It is the coldest, windiest, and driest place on Earth, with no permanent residents, only scientists in research stations.

POLAR TUNDRA

a frigid, treeless area close to the Arctic and Antarctic, where only tiny plants like moss and lichen can survive the brief summer and the ground is permanently frozen.

POLAR ICECAP

a thick blanket of ice covering land or water close to the North or South Pole that remains frozen all year long and has very little flora or fauna.

HIGHLAND CLIMATES

A polar climate is one in which there are long, frigid winters and brief, cool summers, and intense cold throughout the year.

Mount Everest is a highland, it is the tallest mountain on Earth

Standing at 8,848 meters above sea level

the average temperature ranges from -26°C in winter and 18°C in summer.
